Featured Project: Line Following Robot

By: Maggelan, Kavinesh, Parikshet, Paarthav (ATL Gyan Mela)

About the Robot

A Line Following Robot is an autonomous mobile machine designed to detect and follow a path, typically a visible line (like a black line on a white surface) drawn on the floor. It's a fundamental concept in robotics, often used for educational purposes and simple industrial applications.

How It Works: The Logic Flow

1

START & Look

Turns on, gets ready, and uses its sensors to check where the line is right now.

2

Decision Point

Checks if the line is exactly in the middle compared to its body using eyes (sensors).

3

Steering Action

If on line: **Go Straight**. If line is left/right: **Turn Left/Right** to move back over the path.

4

Re-check & End

Stops and wiggles if the line is lost. Checks again after every move. Stops when the finish line is reached.

Key Materials Used

  • Arduino Uno (The Brain)
  • 4 Wheels & 4 Motors (Movement)
  • Lithium Battery Holders & 3.7V Batteries
  • Foamboard (Chassis)
  • Breadboard & Jumper Wires (Circuitry)

Future Benefits & Applications

1. Logistics & Manufacturing

Automating movement of goods (AGVs) in factories to deliver parts, eliminating waiting time and maximizing efficiency.

2. Hospitals & Healthcare

Automating sensitive internal delivery jobs, like moving biohazardous materials, increasing hygiene and safety.

3. Agriculture & Farming

Autonomous planters following GPS paths to ensure perfect seed depth and spacing, increasing crop yield and reducing waste.

4. Safety & Inspection

Robots patrolling high-radiation or toxic areas in chemical plants to check equipment and look for leaks, protecting human workers.

Gyan Mela 2025: Event Overview

The Gyan Mela (Fair of Knowledge) is the annual event where New Horizon Public School students showcase their practical projects. Every student participates, demonstrating core concepts across all subjects—from advanced Physics to complex Mathematics.

It is a foundational exercise in practical application, critical thinking, and communication, transforming students into expert presenters.

Day 1: Internal Review & Preparation
  • Internal review of all projects by faculty and staff.
  • Final practice presentations among student teams.
  • Preparation of displays for high visitor volume on Day 2.
Day 2: Parent & Public Showcase
  • **Open Invitation** to parents and the community.
  • Visitors interact directly with student presenters.
  • Celebration of knowledge and practical learning achievements.
